Afro-Atlantic Research Portals

External collections and discovery resources

Explore selected databases, archives, digital collections, finding aids, and scholarly portals for research on Africa and the Afro-Atlantic world. Each entry identifies what the resource is best for, its geographic coverage, available languages, material types, and access model.

These resources are maintained by independent institutions and are not holdings of the Iroko Historical Society. IHS provides these links as a research service.

Inclusion indicates research relevance, not endorsement of an institution's terminology, custodial history, access practices, or interpretation of the communities represented.

Digital library

Digital Library of the Caribbean

Florida International University · University of Florida · University of the Virgin Islands
A multi-institutional cooperative providing open access to Caribbean and circum-Caribbean cultural, historical, and research materials, including newspapers, maps, and partner archives. Parent portal for several specialized collections, including the Vodou Archive and MANIOC.
Best forCaribbean newspapers, maps, books, and partner archives.
LanguagesEnglish, Spanish, French, and Dutch; varies by collection.
MaterialsNewspapers, maps, books, photographs, and archival collections.
Coverage: Caribbean, circum-Caribbean · Last verified: July 2026
Open digital access Visit collection ↗
Religion collection

Vodou Archive

University of Florida & Duke University · NEH-funded
Documents, photographs, audio, and video recordings pertaining to Haitian Vodou religion and culture. Materials include facsimiles, translations, and transcriptions curating primary sources on Vodou practice.
Best forHaitian Vodou primary sources and ritual-cultural multimedia.
LanguagesEnglish, French, and Haitian Creole; varies by item.
MaterialsDocuments, photographs, audio, video, translations, and transcriptions.
Search termsVodou, vodoun, Haiti, lwa, ritual, sacred arts.
Coverage: Haiti, Haitian diaspora · Last verified: July 2026
Open digital access Visit collection ↗
Digital archive

Slave Societies Digital Archive

Vanderbilt University
More than 700,000 digitized ecclesiastical and secular records documenting Africans and their descendants in Brazil, Cuba, Colombia, Florida, Angola, and Benin from the sixteenth through twentieth centuries. Searchable by collection, map, and named projects, including the Pierre Verger Manuscript from Benin.
Best forEcclesiastical and civil records on Africans and their descendants.
LanguagesPortuguese, Spanish, English, and French; varies by collection.
MaterialsBaptism, marriage, burial, notarial, and secular records.
Search termsSlavery, enslaved Africans, baptism, marriage, burial, ecclesiastical records.
Coverage: Brazil, Cuba, Colombia, Florida, Angola, Benin · Last verified: July 2026
Open digital access Visit collection ↗
Research guide

African Diaspora Subject Guide

Smithsonian National Anthropological Archives
A curated subject guide identifying archival collections across the Smithsonian's holdings relevant to the African Diaspora, linking into National Anthropological Archives and SOVA finding aids. A starting point for locating dispersed manuscript and recording collections rather than a collection in its own right.
Best forLocating dispersed Smithsonian archival collections.
LanguagesEnglish metadata; source languages vary.
MaterialsSubject guide entries, finding aids, and archival collection records.
Search termsAfrican diaspora, National Anthropological Archives, SOVA, finding aids, bibliography.
Coverage: Pan-African diaspora · Last verified: July 2026

Digital library

African Online Digital Library

Michigan State University · Matrix
An open-access library of digitized African cultural heritage materials, developed with museums, archives, and communities across the continent. Organized into thematic galleries covering oral history, Islam in West Africa, and apartheid-era South Africa.
Best forAfrican cultural heritage galleries and oral-history projects.
LanguagesEnglish metadata; source languages vary.
MaterialsOral histories, photographs, museum objects, archival objects, and teaching galleries.
Coverage: Africa, West Africa emphasis · Last verified: July 2026
Open digital access Visit collection ↗
Music and oral history

Lomax Digital Archive

Association for Cultural Equity
Free access to seven decades of field recordings, photographs, and film compiled by Alan Lomax and John A. Lomax, including Caribbean fieldwork from Haiti, the Bahamas, and Trinidad. A primary source base for Afro-Atlantic music, dance, and oral tradition.
Best forField recordings of Afro-Atlantic music, dance, and oral tradition.
LanguagesEnglish metadata; source languages vary by recording.
MaterialsAudio, photographs, film, interviews, and field notes.
Coverage: Caribbean, US South, global · Last verified: July 2026
Open digital access Visit collection ↗
Digital library

Digital Schomburg

New York Public Library · Schomburg Center for Research in Black Culture
Curated digital exhibitions, digitized books, photographs, and oral histories on the global Black experience, produced by Schomburg Center staff and scholars. Broader than Medjat's Afro-Atlantic focus, but a strong entry point for cultural and intellectual context.
Best forBlack diaspora digital exhibitions and digitized cultural context.
LanguagesEnglish primarily.
MaterialsExhibitions, digitized books, photographs, and oral histories.
Coverage: African Diaspora, broadly · Last verified: July 2026

Digital library

Louisiana Digital Library

LSU Libraries · Louisiana Digital Consortium
More than 400,000 digitized items from Louisiana archives, libraries, and museums, including photographs, maps, manuscripts, and oral histories. Hosts the digitized collections of several Gulf South partner institutions, including the Amistad Research Center.
Best forLouisiana and Gulf South digitized archives.
LanguagesEnglish primarily, with some French and Spanish materials.
MaterialsPhotographs, maps, manuscripts, newspapers, and oral histories.
Coverage: Louisiana, Gulf South · Last verified: July 2026
Open digital access Visit collection ↗
Archive

Amistad Research Center

Independent archive · New Orleans
The nation's oldest and largest independent archive specializing in African American history, the African Diaspora, and the Modern Civil Rights Movement, holding over 33,000 books and extensive manuscript, art, and audiovisual collections. Selected materials are digitized through the Louisiana Digital Library; most of the collection requires an onsite research appointment.
Best forAfrican American history, Gulf South, and civil rights archival research.
LanguagesEnglish primarily.
MaterialsBooks, manuscripts, art, audiovisual collections, and onsite holdings.
Coverage: Gulf South, African American history · Last verified: July 2026
Partial digital access · onsite by appointment Visit collection ↗
Digital library

MANIOC

Université des Antilles · Université de Guyane consortium
An open-access digital library covering the Caribbean, Amazonia, and the Guyana Plateau, built by roughly twenty partner institutions in the French Antilles, Guyana, and mainland France. Includes primary sources on slavery and ethnobotany alongside digitized books, periodicals, and images.
Best forFrench Caribbean, Amazonia, and Guiana digitized sources.
LanguagesFrench primarily; source languages vary.
MaterialsBooks, periodicals, images, and primary sources.
Coverage: French Caribbean, Amazonia, Guiana · Last verified: July 2026
Open digital access Visit collection ↗
Archive

Endangered Archives Programme

British Library · funded by Arcadia
Grants and hosts digitized copies of endangered archival collections worldwide, with digitization equipment and training kept in the country of origin under the program's partnership model. Since 2004 it has digitized more than sixteen million images from over ninety countries, including numerous Afro-Atlantic and West African collections.
Best forEndangered archival collections and digitized global primary sources.
LanguagesCollection-dependent.
MaterialsDigitized manuscripts, photographs, newspapers, and administrative records.
Coverage: Global, incl. Africa, Caribbean, Latin America · Last verified: July 2026
Open digital access Visit collection ↗
Archive

Avery Research Center · Lowcountry Digital Library

College of Charleston
Archival and museum collections documenting African American history in Charleston and the South Carolina Lowcountry, including the Gullah Geechee region, held at the site of the former Avery Normal Institute. Digitized holdings are accessible through the Lowcountry Digital Library; the full collection is available onsite.
Best forLowcountry African American and Gullah Geechee archives.
LanguagesEnglish primarily.
MaterialsArchival collections, photographs, museum records, and digitized items.
Coverage: Charleston, SC Lowcountry, Gullah Geechee · Last verified: July 2026
Partial digital access · onsite research Visit collection ↗
Thesis repository

BDTD - Brazilian Digital Library of Theses and Dissertations

Instituto Brasileiro de Informacao em Ciencia e Tecnologia (IBICT)
Brazilian national discovery portal aggregating theses and dissertations from participating universities and research institutions. Strong first stop for Portuguese-language work on Candomble, Umbanda, Afro-Brazilian history, slavery, ethnobotany, and religious studies.
Best forBrazilian graduate research and university repository records.
LanguagesPrimarily Portuguese, with abstracts and metadata varying by institution.
MaterialsTheses, dissertations, abstracts, metadata, and institutional full-text links.
Search termsreligioes afro-brasileiras, Candomble, Umbanda, escravidao, etnobotanica.
Coverage: Brazil; Brazilian researchers abroad · Last verified: July 2026
Open digital access Visit collection ↗

Botanical database

SERNEC Portal

Consortium of 233 herbaria, southeastern United States
Specimen images and metadata from 233 herbaria across 14 southeastern states, spanning roughly 150 years of botanical collection. Useful for identifying and cross-referencing Gulf South plant species documented in hoodoo and rootwork tradition.
Best forSoutheastern U.S. herbarium specimens and plant distribution.
LanguagesScientific names with English metadata.
MaterialsSpecimen images, herbarium metadata, and georeferenced records.
Coverage: Southeastern US, Gulf South · Last verified: July 2026
Open digital access Visit collection ↗
Botanical database

PROTA4U · Plant Resources of Tropical Africa

Originally an international documentation programme, active 2000–2013
A searchable database of roughly 7,000 useful plants of tropical Africa, covering food, medicine, dye, fiber, and other traditional uses by scientific name, common name, and country. A direct companion resource to Pierre Verger's Yoruba ethnobotanical fieldwork underlying the Ewé Database.
Best forTropical African useful plants by scientific or common name.
LanguagesEnglish interface and botanical terminology.
MaterialsPlant profiles, use categories, distribution notes, and references.
Search termsYoruba plants, medicinal plants, useful plants, ethnobotany, botanical names.
Coverage: Tropical Africa, West Africa · Last verified: July 2026
Open digital access · legacy dataset Visit collection ↗
Botanical database

Useful Tropical Plants Database

Independent, volunteer-maintained (Ken Fern & Ajna Fern)
An independently maintained, fully-referenced database of edible, medicinal, and other traditional uses for more than 13,000 tropical and subtropical plant species. A useful cross-referencing tool for ethnobotanical uses recorded in the Ewé Database and Caribbean herbalism sources.
Best forTraditional uses and references for tropical and subtropical species.
LanguagesEnglish.
MaterialsPlant profiles, uses, references, and distribution notes.
Coverage: Pantropical, incl. Caribbean and Africa · Last verified: July 2026
